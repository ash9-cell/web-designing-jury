  Welcome to walnut                    wood carving 

Kashmiri walnut wood carving is one of the well known traditional handicrafts from the Kashmir region of Jammu and Kashmir, India. It is a complex process of hand carving intricate details like patterns and motifs into Walnut Wood by the Kashmiri Karigars (Artisans). Kashmir’s Walnut carving is famous worldwide.

Walnut wood is the preferred most for carving due to its balanced hardness, intricate grain patterns, stability, ease of workability, and capacity to hold fine details. Additionally, its acceptance of stains and finishes, and natural resistance to decay make it an ideal material for creating exquisite and durable products Another factor being the wide availability of walnut trees, in the region. At an altitude of 5500–7500 feet above sea level, Kashmir is one of the very few places in the world that still produces walnuts.
